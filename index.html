Project Reflections (PPMR)

PLAN:

Initial Goal: establish a foundation in Unity and learn basics of game development and scripting.


At the start of the project, I kind of felt overwhelmed by the possibilities of game development and wasn't sure where to begin. My direction changed when I saw an instagram reel discussing beginner-friendly projects, which highlighted a Flappy Bird clone project. This was further reinforced when i remembered my mentor having mentioned Flappy Bird as an example pnceduring our meeting. However, I didn't want to settle for a clone. While trying to think of a way to take the next step in my journey, I understood that my art skills were non existent, so I made a choice: I would stick to the simple looks and sprites of the tutorial but focus all on gameplay changes. My goal was to take a monotonous loop and change it into a replayable experience. I planned to add randomized rounds and a progression system to ensure the game remained engaging rather than repetitive.

PERFORM:

Challenge: Moving from "Following a Recipe" to "Designing a System."


Building on top of the existing tutorial code was harder than expected. The basic code just "paused" the game, which wasn't enough for a Shop system. I realized I couldn't just patch it but I had to recreate the structure entirely to accommodate Game States (Playing, Shop, GameOver) and variable Stats (Speed, Gap Size). I had to rewrite the game manager, making things into variables, moving into gamestates which allowed me to create a shop system, then a heart system. I shared my early builds with friends to ask what things they found 'cool' or interesting. I used their input to balance the difficulty, ensuring that my 'freestyled' code actually turned into a fun user experience

MONITOR:

Consultation: Peer Review with friends (Week 12).


I monitored my progress by showing a beta build to friends( ashveen and miles ). They pointed out that the game ramped up too fast—the randomized difficulty scaling made it unfairly difficult by round 3.

Action Taken: I implemented a Heart System (3 lives) to make it more forgiving and used my StatDisplay tool to fine-tune the difficulty multiplier from 1.5x to 1.1x per round, and also let the player have an idea of their stats and play around it

REFLECT:


Looking back, the pivot was the most valuable part of the journey. By choosing to "innovate" on the mechanics, I tested my ability and understanding more than I would have with a simple clone. I moved from a tutorial to creating a game.
