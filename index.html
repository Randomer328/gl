Project Reflections (PPMR)

PLAN: The Ambition

Initial Goal: To design and develop a playable game from scratch using Unity scripting.


Initially, I rated my Innovation attribute at a 2. I didn't know where to start, so when my facilitator mentioned Flappy Bird, I decided to follow a basic tutorial to build a foundation. However, I realized my art skills were limited, so I had a choice: make a bad-looking original game or stick to the tutorial's aesthetic but innovate on gameplay. I chose the latter, pivoting to an "Arcade Evolution" concept (randomized rounds, shops) to make the game replayable and unique every time.

PERFORM: The Struggle

Challenge: Re-architecting a simple tutorial into a complex system.


Building on top of the existing tutorial code was harder than expected. The basic code just "paused" the game, which wasn't enough for a Shop system. I realized I couldn't just patch it; I had to recreate the structure entirely to accommodate Game States (Playing, Shop, GameOver) and variable Stats (Speed, Gap Size). This forced me to leverage my Critical Thinking attribute to decouple the logic and make it scalable.

MONITOR: The Feedback Loop

Consultation: Peer Review with Ashveen Dev Menon (Week 12).


I monitored my progress by showing a beta build to Ashveen. He pointed out that the game ramped up too fast—the randomized difficulty scaling made it unfairly difficult by Round 3.

Action Taken: I implemented a Heart System (3 lives) to make it more forgiving and used my StatDisplay debug tool to fine-tune the difficulty multiplier from 1.5x to 1.1x per round.

REFLECT: The Growth

Outcome: Critical Thinking 4 → 5.


Looking back, the pivot was the most valuable part of the journey. By choosing to "innovate" on mechanics rather than art, I learned far more about Game Architecture than I would have with a simple clone. I successfully moved from "copying a tutorial" to "engineering a system," satisfying my goal to deepen my technical understanding of game development.
